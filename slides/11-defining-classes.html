4) Defining Classes → Collage

  1. Read filenames from CSV; open as images.
  2. Crop to square; scale uniformly.
  3. Assemble a Wiphala-style collage (true-color).

Each image selected to reflect a color’s cultural meaning (e.g., “verde” ↔ natural resources / lithium mine).

  • AI confusion: Gemini: “5×10 grid of 50 unrelated images.”
  • ChatGPT: “Single image of 7 concatenated tiles; generate 10×10.”
  • Student success factor: pipeline orchestration & careful mapping from color→image tile.
“The fourth milestone focused on abstraction and composition CCSCRM (1) . Students read filenames from a CSV, opened those files as images, cropped and scaled them, and then assembled them into a Wiphala-style collage. Each image corresponded to a color’s cultural meaning — for example, a photo of a Bolivian lithium mine representing ‘verde’ as a symbol of natural resources. Students weren’t just coding; they were connecting computation to cultural narratives. The ambiguity here was deliberate. Instead of giving precise pseudocode, we provided example output and told students to ‘visually inspect the diagonal.’ Students had to reason about how pixel data mapped to collage placement. This step completely confounded most AI tools. Gemini misinterpreted the task as a 5×10 grid of unrelated images, while ChatGPT tried to generate a single concatenated image. Students, meanwhile, built pipelines that reflected real programming practice: integrating I/O, data manipulation, and logic into a cohesive whole.”

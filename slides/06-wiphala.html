The Wiphala (concept)

  • Square emblem used as a flag by Indigenous Andean peoples; diagonal color sequencing with row/column offsets.
  • We avoid naming “Wiphala” in prompts; students infer pattern and code it.
  • Pedagogical bridge: 2D indexing, modular arithmetic, sequence rotations.
Also a frame for social/ethical reflection: link between AI data center demand and Andean mineral extraction and labor rights. The symbol’s meaning matters; students connect code to culture. “The Wiphala is a square, multicolored checkerboard flag used to represent Indigenous peoples of the Andes . It symbolizes unity, diversity, and self-determination — and in our context, it also represents an opportunity to engage with computation in a culturally grounded way. It also provided a chance to talk about the real-world consequences of computing. The demand for data centers — which power AI tools like the ones students use — is deeply linked to mineral extraction and labor exploitation in Andean regions
